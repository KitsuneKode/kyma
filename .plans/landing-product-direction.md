# Landing Product Direction

Use this document when briefing a design or frontend agent to build the `/(marketing)` landing page.

## Objective

Position Kyma as a credible `AI Tutor Screener` for tutoring and education companies that need to screen teaching talent with more consistency, less recruiter time, and stronger auditability.

The landing page should help a buyer understand, in under 20 seconds:

- who the product is for
- what problem it solves
- how the workflow works
- why the output is trustworthy
- what action to take next

## Primary Audience

The landing page should be optimized for:

- recruiter ops leads at tutoring companies
- founders or hiring managers at education businesses
- talent or QA leads responsible for tutor quality

Secondary audience:

- internal stakeholders evaluating the product
- candidates trying to understand the product after receiving an invite

Important:

- the homepage is **not** primarily for candidates
- candidate access stays invite-led
- recruiter value should lead the story

## Product Positioning

Kyma is not a generic AI interview bot.

Kyma is a structured screening system for live tutor evaluation.

It is specifically for roles where communication quality matters as much as subject knowledge:

- clarity
- simplification
- patience
- warmth
- adaptability
- student-safe judgment

## Core Promise

Kyma helps tutoring companies replace inconsistent first-round screening with a structured live interview and an evidence-backed review packet.

Short version:

- `Invite candidates`
- `Run a live AI-led tutor screening`
- `Review transcript, evidence, and recommendation`
- `Make faster, more defensible decisions`

## What We Must Communicate

The landing page should make these ideas obvious:

### 1. Tutor screening is different

A good tutor is not just knowledgeable. They must explain clearly, stay patient, adapt to confusion, and communicate with warmth.

### 2. Kyma evaluates live teaching signals

The product runs a realtime screening conversation instead of relying only on resumes, forms, or generic interview notes.

### 3. The output is reviewable

Kyma does not ask teams to trust a black box. Recruiters can inspect transcript evidence, artifacts, and recommendation rationale.

### 4. Human judgment stays in the loop

Kyma accelerates screening and improves consistency. It does not replace the recruiter's final decision.

## What The Landing Must Not Signal

Avoid these frames entirely:

- generic “AI hiring copilot” language
- autonomous hiring claims
- chatbot-first positioning
- exam/proctoring vibes
- consumer tutoring marketplace vibes
- infrastructure-first messaging that leads with LiveKit, Convex, or models

Do not make the page feel like:

- a hackathon demo
- a template SaaS landing page
- a voice bot gimmick
- an avatar toy

## Messaging Hierarchy

The page should tell this story in order:

### 1. Headline

State the product in buyer language.

Recommended direction:

- `Screen tutors with evidence, not guesswork`
- `Hire high-quality tutors with structured live screening`
- `Give recruiter teams a faster, more trustworthy first round`

### 2. Subheadline

Explain the workflow in one sentence:

- live AI-led interview
- structured tutor-specific evaluation
- transcript and evidence-backed recruiter review

### 3. Immediate proof

Show a product preview that makes the workflow feel real:

- recruiter dashboard
- candidate interview flow
- report or evidence view

The proof area should answer:

- this is a real product
- it already supports a complete flow
- it produces something useful for a recruiter

### 4. Why this matters

Spell out the hiring pain:

- resume screens miss communication quality
- interviewer quality varies
- recruiter notes are inconsistent
- borderline candidates take too long to resolve

### 5. How Kyma works

Make the 3-step product loop simple:

1. recruiter creates a screening and sends an invite
2. candidate completes a live structured interview
3. recruiter reviews evidence-backed output

### 6. Trust layer

This is where the page earns seriousness:

- transcript-backed recommendations
- clear rubric dimensions
- reviewable artifacts
- human override
- durable session records

### 7. Outcome framing

Translate features into buyer outcomes:

- faster screening throughput
- more consistent evaluation
- lower recruiter review time
- stronger hiring audit trail

### 8. Final CTA

Close with a buyer action and an exploration action.

## CTA Strategy

For the current app and route structure:

- primary CTA: `/video-demo` or a future booking/demo route
- secondary CTA: `/interviews/demo-invite`
- tertiary/nav CTA: `/admin`

CTA labels should sound product-led, not generic:

- `Watch product demo`
- `Try the candidate flow`
- `Recruiter login`

Do not use placeholder CTAs or vague labels like:

- `Get started`
- `Learn more`
- `Start building`

## Section Plan

This is the recommended landing composition order for the design agent.

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- establish audience
- state the promise
- show the product
- create one clear next step

Required content:

- sharp buyer headline
- one-sentence workflow explanation
- dual CTA
- product preview panel

### 2. Problem / Why Tutor Screening Needs More Than Resumes

Purpose:

- justify why this product category exists

Suggested content:

- tutors need clarity, patience, and adaptability
- traditional first rounds do not capture those well
- manual screening creates inconsistency

### 3. How It Works

Purpose:

- make the flow feel simple and operational

Structure:

1. send invite
2. run live screening
3. review evidence and decide

### 4. What Recruiters Actually Review

Purpose:

- shift from “AI interview” novelty to reviewer confidence

Show:

- transcript snippets
- rubric dimensions
- recommendation state
- notes/copilot support

### 5. Trust And Control

Purpose:

- answer the buyer’s skepticism directly

Required ideas:

- evidence-backed outputs
- manual review remains possible
- invite-controlled access
- durable records and replayability

### 6. Outcomes / ROI

Purpose:

- connect the workflow to business value

Suggested outcomes:

- less recruiter time per candidate
- more consistent first-round screening
- clearer escalation for borderline candidates
- stronger auditability for hiring teams

### 7. FAQ / Objection Handling

Purpose:

- reduce hesitation

Suggested questions:

- does this replace recruiters?
- what signals does Kyma evaluate?
- can recruiters override the recommendation?
- what does the candidate experience feel like?
- how do invites and access control work?

### 8. Final CTA

Purpose:

- repeat the promise
- give a clear next action

## Visual Direction

The landing should feel:

- refined
- premium
- warm
- trustworthy
- structured
- calm

Recommended visual territory:

- editorial enterprise
- premium operations software
- minimal but not sterile
- confident typography
- clear hierarchy
- product-led imagery over decorative illustration

Avoid:

- playful cartoon styling
- synthetic “AI glow” aesthetics
- purple-gradient startup clichés
- over-animated hero effects

## Copy Guardrails

Preferred language:

- structured screening
- live interview
- recruiter review
- evidence-backed recommendation
- tutor quality
- communication and teaching signals

Avoid language like:

- agents replacing recruiters
- autonomous hiring
- instant candidate ranking without review
- magical scoring

## Product Truths The Design Agent Must Preserve

- candidate flow is invite-based
- live interview is the core product loop
- recruiter review is evidence-backed and human-reviewable
- the product is tutor-screening-specific, not generic interviewing
- trust matters more than novelty

## Recommended Design-Agent Brief

When handing this off to a design agent, ask for:

1. a landing page for recruiter-side buyers at tutoring companies
2. a clear hero with buyer-first messaging and product preview
3. a section order that explains the workflow before feature detail overload
4. strong trust framing around transcript evidence, rubric structure, and human review
5. premium but restrained visuals that feel operationally credible
6. CTA strategy centered on demo + candidate-flow exploration

## Success Check

The landing direction is correct if a first-time visitor can accurately say:

- `This helps tutoring companies screen tutors`
- `It runs a live AI-led interview`
- `Recruiters still review evidence before deciding`
- `This looks trustworthy enough to evaluate seriously`
